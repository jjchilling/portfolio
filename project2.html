Partiful: Hangout

Team
Julie Chung, Roberto Gonzales, Emily Wang, Kara Wong
Role
Designer
Skills
UI/UX, Design, Wireframe
Duration
March - April 2025

Overview

Partiful is a mobile app targeted towards college students who host parties or events with friends or classmates. According to the Partiful team, the most popular mean for people to use Partiful is to gather RSVP for an event they feel confident about with a pre-established event idea.

Identifying the Issue

The Partiful team recognized that Partiful can feel too "extra" for smaller hangs, and the users typically resort to chaotic group chats or sharing flyers through Instagram. Another pain point recognized by the users was that they feel responsible for an event, because they must pick a poster / theme / effect and serve as a "host".

Solution to the Problem

Thus, we decided to conduct user research and comparative analysis to determine how to develop a smaller gathering feature for friend groups in college. When spoken with numerous college students that fall within Partiful's target audience, the two most popular competitors of Partiful were Google Calendar and When2Meet. We compared the pros and cons of both competitors and decided to takeaway features that will be beneficial to Partiful.

Based on this comparative analysis, we determined that a core functionality we must implement to make sure users can approach this feature easily was to reduce steps to create a new event in the app and also allow users to view everyone's availability in a more visually appealing manner. Therefore, we decided to name the small gathering feature as Hangout, and incorporated to the pre-existing Partiful's design. We also explored the possibility of adding an iMessage plugin that allows users to directly host events and RSVP immediately from their texts.

For the design process, I began by creating a low-fidelity design emphasizing the key functionalities from the user analysis process. I decided to reference pre-existing movie theater websites, such as AMC and Cinemark. I utilized traditional elements such as vertical layouts, grids, and sliding galleries. Conclusion

This project was extremely insightful, since it was my first time working with a company on a design project. In particular, it was fascinating to devise a solution to a problem that Partiful had.

In particular, being a group of college students, who have used Partiful in the past, this was a great opportunity to be hands on with a product that could benefit more from insight from their target audience.

One of the biggest challenges while working this project was to balance between maintaining the high customization ability that Partiful offers, while simplifying the userflow enough to fit with the purpose of easy, small gatherings.
