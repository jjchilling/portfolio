Overview

Avon Cinema is a movie theater that has been loved by Providence locals and Brown University students for decades. They also host local and student movie festivals, and bring various artistic and commercial films to the area. However, the website was last updated in the 2000s and implements outdated web principles. Therefore, for this project, I have created an interactive redesign of the Avon Cinema official website to increase accessibility for a broader audience.

Identifying the Issue

I began by analyzing the website’s target audience. After inquiring local students about what the primary function of the website is, most responded that they utilize the website to check which movies are currently screening, purchase tickets, and find miscellaneous information such as events and festivals. Thus, in order to maximize learnability and efficiency for the users. I approached the design process by highlighting those functionalities. The first image below depicts the official website of Avon Cinema, and the second image addresses accessibility and readability issues according to the WAVE AIM tool.

Design Process

Lo-fi Designs

For the design process, I began by creating a low-fidelity design emphasizing the key functionalities from the user analysis process. I decided to reference pre-existing movie theater websites, such as AMC and Cinemark. I utilized traditional elements such as vertical layouts, grids, and sliding galleries. The following sliding galleries depict my low-fidelity design for desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Hi-fi Designs

Based on the low-fidelity design, I have created a high-fidelity design that incorporates the original website's visual elements and pictures. The biggest difference from the low-fidelity design comes from the vertical image of the theater that spans throughout the website behind the opaque black background to encapsulate the visual intent of the original website.

I began by determining the main color palette for the website, various button designs, and icons to use. I focused on incorporating colors from the website's image to maintain a sense of coherency throughout the page.

Final Prototype

Conclusion

This project allowed me to implement my skills regarding responsive design, analyze users need for a website, and develop a webpage that is used often by local community and students. By undergoing preliminary research and iterating multiple rounds of design stages, I was able to holistically consider the needs of Avon Cinema and the users and ensure that the page was accessible, responsive, and easy to use. If I were to visit this project again in the future, I would love to add unique elements to tablet and mobile designs, and expand upon other pages the website has.
